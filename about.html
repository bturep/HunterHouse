About the Foundation

Victoria, British Columbia · Est. 2026 · Not-for-profit

The Hunter House Foundation stewards the residence at 203 Goward Road and the archival legacy of its architect. Its overarching aim is to realize the speculative futures embedded in Hunter's drawings — among them a cantilevered Skywalk and the unfinished East Wing developments — and to open the archive to scholars, students, and the public.

Mandatethree columns of work

01 / Stewardship

Preserve the residence

Maintain the building and grounds at 203 Goward Road as a working architectural environment — habitable, photographable, available to researchers under defined conditions.

02 / Catalogue

Publish the archive

Catalogue every item under a permanent identifier scheme. Maintain the structured catalogue as a Wikibase. Open the data.

03 / Realize

Build what was drawn

Where the drawings record speculative additions that were never built — the cantilevered Skywalk, the unbuilt East Wing rooms, the Zen scheme — investigate the feasibility of completing them.

Student Fellowship

The Hunter House Student Fellowship is a pilot fellowship supporting one or two students per semester on research, design, writing, or art engaging the archive. Open to undergraduate and graduate students working at the intersections Hunter himself moved through — architecture and its adjacent disciplines: art history, Zen, vernacular and alternative communities, organic design. Each fellowship culminates in a deliverable that enters the archive.
