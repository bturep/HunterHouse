About the Foundation.

Victoria, British Columbia · Est. 2024 · Not-for-profit

The Hunter House Foundation stewards the residence and the archival legacy of its architect. Its overarching aim is to realize the speculative futures embedded in Hunter's drawings — among them a cantilevered Skywalk and the unfinished East Wing developments — and to open the archive to scholars, students and the public.

01  Mandatethree columns of work

01 / Stewardship

Preserve the residence

Maintain the building and grounds at 203 Goward Road as a working architectural environment — habitable, photographable, available to researchers under defined conditions.

02 / Catalogue

Publish the archive

Catalogue every item — drawings, photographs, correspondence, engineering documents — under a permanent identifier scheme. Maintain the structured catalogue as a Wikibase. Open the data.

03 / Realize

Build what was drawn

Where the drawings record speculative additions that were never built — the cantilevered Skywalk, the unbuilt East Wing rooms, the Zen dojo scheme — investigate the feasibility of completing them.

03  Fellowship

The Hunter House Fellowship

A residency program for one architect, scholar or writer per year, working from the residence with access to the archive. Open to applicants from any country. The fellowship covers travel, lodging at the residence, and a modest stipend for three months of residency.
