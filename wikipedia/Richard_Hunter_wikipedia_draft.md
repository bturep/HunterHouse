## PART 1 — DRAFT ARTICLE (paste-ready Wikipedia markup)

```wikitext
'''Richard Morrow Hunter''' (1930 – 14 January 2023) was an American-Canadian architect associated with the American organic architecture tradition. Trained at the University of Colorado and the University of Oklahoma, he practised in California and Alaska before relocating to Victoria, British Columbia, in 1968. He is best known for his own residence above Prospect Lake in Saanich, which he designed and continuously developed over five decades, from 1970 until 2020. He was also a published authority on the early sketches of the German architect [[Erich Mendelsohn]].<ref name="structurist" />

== Early life and education ==
Hunter was born in 1930. He studied architecture at the University of Colorado and at the University of Oklahoma School of Architecture, where he was among the students taught under [[Bruce Goff]] during the period later characterised as the "American School".<ref name="segger">{{cite news |last=Segger |first=Martin |title=Architecture of place |work=The British Columbia Review |date=25 October 2024 |url=https://thebcreview.ca/2024/10/25/2337-segger-architecture/ |access-date=2 June 2026}}</ref><ref name="renegades">{{cite book |editor1-last=Guido |editor1-first=Luca |editor2-last=Pilat |editor2-first=Stephanie |editor3-last=Person |editor3-first=Angela |title=Renegades: Bruce Goff and the American School of Architecture |location=Norman |publisher=University of Oklahoma Press |year=2020 |isbn=978-0-8061-6460-1 |page=228}}</ref>

== Career ==
Hunter practised architecture in California and Alaska before relocating to Victoria, British Columbia, in 1968.<ref name="caa">{{cite web |title=Richard Hunter fonds |website=Canadian Architectural Archives, University of Calgary |id=CA ACU CAA F0076 |url=https://searcharchives.ucalgary.ca/richard-hunter-accession |access-date=2 June 2026}}</ref> Though a long-time Victoria architect, his professional output was modest in number; his principal work was his own residence.<ref name="segger" />

== The Hunter Residence ==
From 1970, Hunter designed and built his own residence on a rocky knoll above Prospect Lake in Saanich, British Columbia. He remained its sole architect, client, and resident, producing drawings for the house continuously until 2020. Reviewers have noted the craftsmanship of its finishes, fittings, and built-in furniture as the distinguishing feature of the house.<ref name="segger" /><ref name="tc2006">{{cite news |last=Westad |first=Kim |title=Forever natural |work=Times Colonist |location=Victoria, British Columbia |date=23 October 2006}}</ref> The residence is documented in the scholarly study ''Renegades: Bruce Goff and the American School of Architecture'' (2020).<ref name="renegades" />

== Mendelsohn scholarship ==
Hunter was a scholar of the German architect [[Erich Mendelsohn]], particularly his early architectural sketches. He co-authored, with the architectural historian Ita Heinze-Greenberg, the article "On the Early Sketches of Eric Mendelsohn".<ref name="structurist">{{cite journal |last1=Heinze-Greenberg |first1=Ita |last2=Hunter |first2=Richard |title=On the Early Sketches of Eric Mendelsohn |journal=The Structurist |issue=45/46 |year=2005 |pages=69–75}}</ref> He is among the individuals represented in the Erich and Luise Mendelsohn papers held at the [[Getty Research Institute]],<ref name="getty">{{cite web |title=Erich and Luise Mendelsohn papers, 1894–1992 |website=Getty Research Institute |id=Accession no. 880406 |url=https://www.getty.edu/research/collections/collection/113YN4 |access-date=2 June 2026}}</ref> and his correspondence on Mendelsohn's drawings featured in a 2004 Mendelsohn symposium at the Manchester School of Architecture.<ref name="cube">{{cite news |title=Mendelsohn Symposium |work=Urban Realm |date=15 October 2004 |url=https://www.urbanrealm.com/news/287/Mendelsohn_Symposium.html |access-date=2 June 2026}}</ref>

== Exhibitions ==
Hunter's work was included in the exhibition ''From the Ground, Into the Light: Organic Architecture of the Islands, 1950–2000'' at the Wentworth Villa Architectural Heritage Museum, Victoria, British Columbia (27 June – 12 October 2024).<ref name="segger" />

== Archive ==
Hunter's papers are held as the Richard Hunter fonds at the [[Canadian Architectural Archives]], University of Calgary (reference CA ACU CAA F0076). The fonds comprises some 344 architectural drawings, photographs, presentation panels, and textual records documenting his student work at the University of Oklahoma and his practice in California, Alaska, and British Columbia.<ref name="caa" />

== Death ==
Hunter died in Victoria, British Columbia, on 14 January 2023.<ref name="segger" />
