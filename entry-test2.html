Letter L001 · Hunter to Luise Mendelsohn · 13 September 1970

“… I am taking the day off from my most recent job of being ankle deep in the dirt of the forty foot square ugly excavation for a little house we'll start building next week on our land. Looks like this:”

“Nothing extraordinary in an absolute sense but if it comes off as I expect the total cost of less than $12,000 will make it very extraordinary. I never thought I'd do anything so simple and boxy but here it is! Inside and out covered alike with cedar boards of various widths and thicknesses. Irregular window widths — rhythm of tree trunks in a forest. Large Japanese style bath and central glass domed garden. I'll do all the finishing as well as manufacturing (manual) of doors and windows . . . (corner beams are rain scuppers)”

— Richard Hunter to Luise Mendelsohn · 13 September 1970 · Letter L001

Richard Morrow Hunter

1930 – 2023 · Architect

Trained at the University of Colorado and the University of Oklahoma, Hunter practised in California and Alaska before relocating to Victoria, British Columbia, in 1968.

His residence at 203 Goward Road occupied him from 1970 until 2020 — five decades of continuous design on a single site. Across those years he remained its sole architect, sole client, and sole resident. The working file he kept of its drawings is the principal subject of this Foundation.
