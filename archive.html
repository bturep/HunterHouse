§ 02 A catalogue of a single house

About the archive.

147 items · 6 source collections · 9 item types · 1970 — 2020

The Hunter House Archive is a structured catalogue of drawings, photographs, and related materials documenting the design and continuous revision of the Hunter Residence. It draws from six source collections, each with a distinct provenance.

01  The collectionssix provenances

The archive recognizes six source collections. The complementary character of the two largest is significant: Hunter donated his original 1970–74 build documentation to the Canadian Architectural Archives; what he kept were the working files for everything that came after. The two collections describe the same building from opposite ends of its lifespan.

HHC 110 items

Hunter House Collection

Working drawings retained by Hunter — the post-build record. Speculative sketches alongside permit submissions, undated schemes alongside dated construction documents. 1975–2020.
CAA 18 items

Canadian Architectural Archives

The original 1970–74 Scheme 1A drawings and early photographs, donated by Hunter in 1985. Held by the University of Calgary, fonds F0076.
FUL 9 items

Fulker Collection

Professional photographs of the original 1974 structure, taken by William Fulker prior to the West Wing addition.
SNY 4 items

Snyder Correspondence

Letters between Hunter and the poet Gary Snyder, 1977–1983. Concerns the Zen dojo scheme and the broader question of regional architectural language.
ENG 5 items

Engineering Drawings

Drawings commissioned by Hunter from Harold Engineering and Brown and Grant Engineering for the East Wing structural conditions on the bluff.
MIS 1 item

Miscellaneous

Material that doesn't fit the other five, retained for context — a 2008 sketch on the back of an envelope, the 1986 building inspector's letter.

02  How items are identifiedarchive ID schema

Every item in the archive receives a permanent, unique Archive ID at the time of ingestion. Archive IDs are permanent — once assigned, an ID is never reused, reassigned, or altered. The number after the dash is a four-digit zero-padded sequence; the letter before it identifies the type of material.

03  How the catalogue workscontrolled vocabulary

The archive is maintained as a structured database with a controlled vocabulary. Drawings are classified by type (plan, elevation, section, detail, framing plan), by use (construction, design development, schematic), by built status, and by the project set they belong to. Areas of the house are tagged from a hierarchical vocabulary of thirty-five terms.

A small selection of those terms:

Elevation Plan Section Detail Pencil study Coloured study Framing plan East Wing West Wing Tower Skywalk Studio Kitchen Dining Entry Garden Kotatsu Design Development Construction Permit Schematic Built Partially built Unbuilt Removed

The structured catalogue allows the archive to be read at multiple resolutions. A drawing can be located by date, by area, by phase, by drawing type, or by the project set it belongs to. The same drawing appears in multiple browsable indexes.

04  Access

The catalogue is open and queryable. The data is published as a Wikibase, supporting both browsing through this site and direct SPARQL queries against the underlying graph. Image files are released under a Public — Hunter House Foundation license unless an individual item carries different rights metadata.
