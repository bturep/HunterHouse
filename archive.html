The Archive

147 items · 6 source collections · 9 item types · 1970 — 2020

The Hunter House Archive is a structured catalogue of drawings, photographs, correspondence, and related materials assembled from six source collections. It is hosted on Wikibase, the open-source structured-data platform that underlies Wikidata, and is openly queryable through SPARQL and a JSON API. Browse the full catalogue · 147 items →

Source collectionssix provenances

The archive draws from six collections of varied provenance — two institutional, four held by individuals connected to Hunter's life and practice. Each is described below. None of these cards links elsewhere; collections will receive their own finding-aid pages as cataloguing completes.

Archive identifierspermanent ID schema

Every item receives a permanent, unique Archive ID at ingestion. IDs are never reused, reassigned, or altered. The letter identifies the material; the four-digit sequence is zero-padded and runs in order of cataloguing.

Controlled vocabulary

Items are classified by type, by use, by built status, and by the project set they belong to. Areas of the house are tagged from a hierarchical vocabulary of thirty-five terms. The same item appears in multiple indexes — it can be located by date, area, phase, drawing type, or project set.

Platform & accessWikibase

The archive is hosted on Wikibase, the open-source structured-data platform that underlies Wikidata. Items, properties, and statements are stored as a graph and queried via SPARQL or a JSON API. The Foundation maintains its instance at hunterhouse.wikibase.cloud; this site reads from it directly.

Image files are released under a Public — Hunter House Foundation license unless an individual item carries different rights metadata. Researchers wishing to consult high-resolution master files or unpublished collections should contact the project lead — see About.
